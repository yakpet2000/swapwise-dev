You Don't Have to Sell to Diversify.

The IRS has a legal path that lets you keep your full investment working for you — instead of writing a check to the government. Here's how it works.

Who This Is For

Sound Familiar?

Millions of people are sitting on large stock positions they can't sell without a massive tax hit. If any of these sound like you, keep reading.

Long-Term Employee

You've accumulated company stock over years through RSUs, ESPP, or options. It's done well — but now 40%, 60%, or more of your net worth is in one ticker. Selling means a six-figure tax bill.

Inherited Stock

You inherited shares from a parent or grandparent. The stock has grown significantly since, and you'd like to spread the risk — but capital gains taxes make that expensive.

Too Much in One Stock

Maybe you bought early in a company that took off, or your portfolio has drifted over time. Either way, too much of your wealth depends on one company's future.

How It Works

A Legal Path to Diversification — Without Selling

Section 351 of the IRS tax code allows you to contribute stock into a new, diversified fund and receive shares of that fund in return — without owing taxes on the exchange. Here's the process, in plain English.

1

A New Fund Opens

A fund manager files to create a brand-new ETF and opens a window where investors can contribute stock before it launches.

2

You Contribute Your Stock

You transfer your appreciated shares — along with other investors doing the same — into the new fund. No sale happens, so no taxes are triggered.

3

You Receive ETF Shares

In exchange, you get shares of the new ETF equal in value to what you put in. You now own a diversified fund instead of a single stock.

4

Sell Whenever You Want

There's no lockup period on most 351 ETFs. You can hold long-term or sell ETF shares on the open market whenever it suits you.

⚠️

Important to Know

This defers your taxes — it doesn't eliminate them. You'll owe capital gains when you eventually sell your ETF shares. Our eligibility check below will tell you if your portfolio qualifies.
